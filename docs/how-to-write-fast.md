# How to Write Fast

ClinLang is built for speed. Do not type full sentences unless you are in the History of Present Illness. Instead, combine your words and numbers.

## Typing Patient Details
Don't use spaces. Combine the numbers and letters together.
*   **Age and Sex**: Type `58M` or `32F`. 
*   **Weight**: Attach `wt` to the number. Type `wt70`.
*   **Height**: Attach `ht` to the number. Type `ht170`.
*   **Example**: `pt 58M wt70 ht170`

## Symptom Tracking (+ and -)
Instead of explaining how bad a symptom is, just attach pluses (`+`) or minuses (`-`) to the end of the symptom.

*   `+` = Mild or present
*   `++` = Severe
*   `+++` = Very severe
*   `-` = Improving
*   `--` = Completely resolved

**Examples:**
*   `fever++`
*   `pain---`
*   `nausea+`

You can also attach time right after the severity using `h` (hours) or `d` (days):
*   `pain+++4h` (Very severe pain for 4 hours)
*   `cough++3d` (Severe cough for 3 days)

## Using the Colon (:)
If you need to write out a finding or a specific test result that has words in it, use a colon. Whatever is on the left is the test, whatever is on the right is the result.
*   `cxr:clear lungs`
*   `cvs:normal s1s2`
*   `widal:1/60`
